## Tema 1: IT Help Desk

1. Welcome to the IT help desk.
2. Please have your student ID ready.
3. Laptops can be borrowed for up to three days.
4. You must connect to the secure student network.
5. Software updates run automatically every weekend.
6. If you forget your password, use the self-service portal to reset it.
7. Late equipment returns result in a temporary suspension of your borrowing privileges.
8. We can help you connect to the campus network.
9. Please restart your laptop before we run any diagnostics.
10. Enter your university email address to access the system.
11. The password is the same one you use for course registration.
12. Wireless internet is available in all classroom buildings on campus.
13. If your device keeps disconnecting, you may need to update your operating system.
14. Students can download free antivirus software from the main technology portal.
15. Contact the IT office by phone, email, or walk-in during business hours.

## Tema 2: Computer Lab

16. Welcome to the main computer lab.
17. Please sign in at the front desk.
18. Color printing costs ten cents per page.
19. You must have a valid student ID to log in to any computer.
20. Do not leave your personal belongings unattended in the lab.
21. If you need help with the software, ask the assistant at the main desk.
22. The lab stays open twenty-four hours during the final exam period.
23. Save your work to an external drive — computers clear all data daily.
24. Technical support staff is on duty at the front counter until midnight.
25. You can book a private workstation on the university website in advance.
26. Food and drinks are not allowed near the machines to protect the equipment.
27. Printing requires a positive balance on your student account.
28. Do not save personal files on the lab desktops.
29. Log out completely when you finish to protect your account.
30. Software on these machines is updated automatically every Sunday night.

## Tema 3: Campus Parking

31. Bring your student ID to the front desk to apply for a parking permit.
32. Complete the parking application online before visiting the office.
33. Park only in the zones assigned to your specific permit type.
34. Place the permit sticker on the lower left corner of your windshield.
35. Commuter students may not leave their cars overnight in the main lot.
36. If you lose your parking pass, pay the replacement fee before getting a new one.
37. Visitors should buy a daily pass from the kiosk near the main campus entrance.
38. All vehicles must display a valid permit during posted enforcement hours.
39. Illegally parked vehicles will be ticketed and may be towed.
40. Handicapped spaces require a state-issued placard or university permit.
41. Permit fees are charged to your student account at the start of each semester.
42. Report damaged parking meters or gate malfunctions to the transit office.
43. Carpooling permits are available at a reduced rate through the transportation office.
44. Electric vehicle charging stations are located in lots B and D.
45. The transit office is open weekdays from seven in the morning until six in the evening.
