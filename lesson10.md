## Senaryo 1: IT Help Desk — Orientation

1. Welcome to the IT help desk.
2. Please show your student ID.
3. Sign in at the front desk.
4. Laptops can be borrowed for up to three days.
5. Personal devices must be registered to access the secure network.
6. If you forget your password, use the self-service portal on the university website to reset it.
7. Returning equipment late will result in a temporary suspension of your borrowing privileges for the semester.

---

## Senaryo 2: IT Help Desk — Network & Software

8. Please restart your laptop first.
9. Enter your university email address.
10. The IT desk is open weekdays.
11. The password is the same one you use for course registration.
12. If your device disconnects often, you may need to update your operating system.
13. Wireless internet is available in all classroom and administrative buildings across the entire campus.
14. Students can download free antivirus software directly from the university technology portal at no charge.

---

## Senaryo 3: Computer Lab — Basic Use

15. Welcome to the computer lab.
16. Please sign in at the desk.
17. Log out when you are done.
18. You must use your student ID to log in to any computer.
19. Color printing costs ten cents per page from your student account.
20. Personal files must be saved to an external drive because computers erase all data when you log out.
21. If you need help with the software, ask the technical support assistant at the front counter for guidance.

---

## Senaryo 4: Computer Lab — Rules & Hours

22. Do not leave items unattended.
23. Save your work often.
24. Food is not allowed here.
25. The lab is open twenty-four hours during final exams each semester.
26. You can reserve a workstation online up to twenty-four hours in advance.
27. Drinks and food are strictly prohibited near the computers and printers to protect the equipment from damage.
28. Software on all lab computers is updated automatically each Sunday night to keep the systems current and secure.

---

## Senaryo 5: Campus Parking — Applying for a Permit

29. Complete the application online first.
30. Bring your student ID to the office.
31. Place the sticker on your windshield.
32. You must park only in the zones assigned to your specific permit type.
33. Commuter students are not permitted to leave their vehicles in the main lot overnight.
34. If you lose your parking pass, you will need to pay the replacement fee before receiving a new one.
35. Visitors without a permit should purchase a daily pass from the kiosk located near the main campus entrance.

---

## Senaryo 6: Campus Parking — Rules & Services

36. A permit is required on campus.
37. Illegally parked cars will be towed.
38. Carpool permits cost less each term.
39. Accessible spaces require a valid state placard or a university disability permit.
40. Permit fees are automatically charged to your student account at the start of each semester.
41. Electric vehicle charging stations are available in parking lots B and D near the main science buildings.
42. The campus transportation office is open weekdays from seven in the morning until six in the evening.
