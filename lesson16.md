## Senaryo 1: Community Garden — Welcome

1. Welcome to the community garden.
2. Please sign in at the shed.
3. Garden tools are stored inside.
4. Each student plot is marked with a small wooden number sign.
5. You may pick your own vegetables once they are fully ripe.
6. Before you leave today, please rinse every tool you used and hang it back on the wall.
7. If you plan to grow tall plants like corn or sunflowers, please use the plots along the back fence.

---

## Senaryo 2: Community Garden — Planting & Care

8. Water your plants early in the morning.
9. Pull the weeds around each row.
10. Add compost to enrich the soil.
11. Seedlings should be spaced a few inches apart so their roots have room to grow.
12. Please do not use any chemical sprays, since this is a fully organic garden.
13. During the hot summer months, the soil dries out quickly, so check the moisture level every single day.
14. If your plants look pale or their leaves start to yellow, they may need more sunlight or a bit of fertilizer.

---

## Senaryo 3: Campus Greenhouse — Tour

15. Welcome to the campus greenhouse.
16. Please keep the door closed behind you.
17. The tropical plants are on the right.
18. We control the temperature and humidity to match each plant's natural climate.
19. The seedlings on these tables will be moved outdoors once the frost season ends.
20. Please never touch the plants unless a staff member has clearly given you permission to do so.
21. This greenhouse is used for student research, so some of the plants are part of ongoing experiments you should not disturb.

---

## Senaryo 4: Grounds Crew — Landscaping

22. The grounds crew starts work at dawn.
23. Freshly cut grass is collected for compost.
24. Please stay off the newly seeded lawn.
25. We plant flower beds in the spring and trim the hedges throughout the summer.
26. Fallen leaves are raked and cleared from the walkways every morning during the autumn.
27. If you notice a broken sprinkler or a fallen branch anywhere on campus, please report it to the grounds office.
28. The trees along the main path are pruned each winter so that they grow strong and healthy in the coming year.

---

## Senaryo 5: Gardening Workshop — Learning

29. Today's workshop is about planting bulbs.
30. Each of you will get a small pot.
31. Fill the pot with potting soil first.
32. Push the bulb down until only its tip is showing above the surface.
33. Water it gently and place the pot on a bright windowsill indoors.
34. In a few weeks you should begin to see a small green shoot pushing up through the soil.
35. If you follow these simple steps carefully at home, your bulbs will bloom into colorful flowers by early spring.

---

## Senaryo 6: Harvest & Farmers Market

36. The harvest festival is next weekend.
37. Fresh vegetables are sold at the market.
38. Volunteers are needed on Saturday morning.
39. All of the produce sold here was grown right here on our own campus garden.
40. Any vegetables that are left over at the end of the day are donated to the local food bank.
41. If you would like to volunteer at the market, please add your name to the sign-up sheet posted near the entrance.
42. Come early if you want the best selection, because the most popular items, like tomatoes and fresh herbs, sell out very quickly.

---

## Senaryo 7: Bahçe Aletleri & Güvenlik — "Emir + Sebep" Kalıbı ⭐

> Bu senaryo, sınavda seni zorlayan tipteki cümleler için: **bir emir + neden**.
> Tekniği: cümleyi ikiye böl → [ne yap] / [neden]. Örn: *"put the hose away" / "so no one trips over it."*

43. Please use the wheelbarrow.
44. Wear gloves while you work.
45. Put the tools back when you finish.
46. After you water the plants, coil the hose so no one trips over it.
47. Please stack the empty pots neatly so they do not blow away in the wind.
48. Before you leave the garden today, lock the shed so that the tools stay safe overnight.
49. When you are done raking, please carry the leaves to the compost bin instead of leaving them on the path.
